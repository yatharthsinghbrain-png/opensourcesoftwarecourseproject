Open source software is not merely a technological paradigm but a structural shift in how knowledge, power and innovation are distributed in the digital world. Today’s computing infrastructure from operating systems to enterprise systems is deeply rooted in collaborative, transparent and community-driven development models.

This report presents a comprehensive audit of LibreOffice, one of the most prominent open-source office suites globally. The analysis extends beyond technical features to examine its origin, governance crisis, licensing framework, ethical implications, Linux ecosystem presence and comparison with proprietary alternatives.

The objective is to understand not just how LibreOffice works but why it exists and what it represents in the broader discourse of software freedom, corporate control and community ownership.
